Privacy Policy

Xavier — Expense Tracker

Last updated: 18 July 2026

The short version: Xavier collects no personal data. Your expenses live only on your iPhone, encrypted at rest. There are no accounts, no tracking, and nothing is sent to us or to any third party.

Who this applies to

This policy covers the Xavier — Expense Tracker app for iOS (“Xavier”, “the app”, “we”, “us”). By using Xavier you agree to this policy.

Data we collect

None. Xavier does not collect, transmit, sell, or share any personal information. We have no servers that receive your data, no analytics or advertising SDKs, and no account system. We cannot see your expenses, because they never leave your device.

Where your data lives

  • Your expenses, accounts, and settings are stored in a local database on your iPhone, encrypted at rest.
  • There is no sign-up and no login. Xavier does not create an account or collect an email address, name, or device identifier.

Backups

If you choose to back up your data, the backup is written to your own iCloud account, managed by Apple under Apple’s privacy policy. Backups never pass through any infrastructure operated by us.

Camera and photos

If you scan a receipt, Xavier uses your camera (or a photo you pick) only to read the receipt on your device. The image and anything read from it are processed locally and are not uploaded to us or anyone else. Access is requested only when you use that feature.

Face ID

You can optionally lock the app with Face ID. Authentication is handled entirely by iOS — your biometric data is never accessed by, or shared with, Xavier.

Third parties

Xavier includes no third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking. No data is shared with third parties because no data is collected in the first place.

Children’s privacy

Xavier is rated 4+ and collects no data from anyone, including children under 13.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, we’ll update this page and revise the “last updated” date above. Material changes will be reflected here before they take effect.
